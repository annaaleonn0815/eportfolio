Development Team Project: Design Document At the end of Unit 6, my team submitted the Design Document for our proposed secure software. The Design Document required us to take on the role of IT Developers to create a secure repository application for an organisation of our choice; we chose the Dutch Internet Forensics organisation as our domain. In this document, our team proposed an application that allows authenticated users to perform CRUD capabilities via a web browser. In this instance, registered users (officers) and non-users are able to delete, create, update, and delete vulnerability reports. To achieve this, our proposed application abides GDPR guidelines by implementing mechanisms of authentication, authorization, data encryption, and event monitoring. System Implementation At the end of Unit 11, our team submitted the final coding output for the application that we proposed in our Design Document in Unit 6. In our final coding output, we created an application that allows the Dutch Internet Forensics officers to perform CRUD capabilities, including functionalities of non-users that includes reporting vulnerabilities to the organisation. Compared to the Design Document, our team had to make a few changes. First, the frontend of the application is no longer in react. Our team chose to use Django because not every team member had experience in React, and Django is Python-based and therefore is a better option for full-stack capabilities. As a result, we made a second change to our application: we used SQLite instead of PostgreSQL, as SQLite is supported by Django and encryption is provided by Django-cryptography library.
